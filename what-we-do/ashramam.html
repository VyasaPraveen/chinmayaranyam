Spiritual Activities

The Heart of Chinmayaranyam

Established by Pujya Shri Mataji Sarada Priyananda, the Ashramam is the spiritual nucleus of Chinmayaranyam, where devotees and seekers gather for daily worship, scriptural study, and selfless service. Rooted in the Vedantic tradition, every activity here is an offering to the Divine.

The Ashram offers programs for all ages — Bala Vihar for children (5–18 yrs), Chinmaya Yuva Kendra (CHYK) for youth (18–30 yrs), daily poojas, bhajans, Jnana Yagnas, and community seva.

Spiritual Activities

• • •

Daily Pooja

Elaborate poojas performed thrice daily with traditional Vedic rituals, offering flowers, fruits, and prayers to the presiding deities.

Bhajans

Soulful devotional singing sessions held every evening, creating an atmosphere of divine bliss and spiritual elevation.

Gita Parayanam

Regular chanting and study of the Bhagavad Gita, the timeless scripture that forms the foundation of Chinmaya Mission's teachings.

Bala Vihar

Weekly sessions for children aged 5 to 18, imparting lessons on Hindu culture, values, and philosophy in an engaging manner.

Chinmaya Yuva Kendra (CHYK)

A youth wing for individuals aged 18 to 30, focusing on harnessing youth potential through dynamic spirituality.

ॐ

Seva Activities

• • •

Annadanam

Free food distribution to devotees, pilgrims, and the needy is conducted daily. Thousands of meals are served every month as a sacred offering, embodying "Anna Daanam Maha Daanam."

Welfare Programs

Regular health camps, clothing distribution, skill development workshops, and support programs for the economically weaker sections of the community.

Educational Support

Scholarships, free study materials, and mentoring for deserving students from underprivileged backgrounds, ensuring access to quality education.

Environmental Initiatives

Tree planting drives, green campus maintenance, and eco-friendly practices that promote harmony with nature in the ashram premises.
