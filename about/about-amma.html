Biography

Pujya Shri Mataji Sarada Priyananda

Pujya Shri Mataji Sarada Priyananda is the visionary behind Chinmayaranyam Ashram. She established and developed this sacred abode near Tirupati, dedicating her life to the propagation of Vedantic wisdom and selfless service under the umbrella of Chinmaya Mission.

During her Brahmacharini days, Mataji conducted Jnana Yagnas (spiritual discourses) not only in Andhra Pradesh but also across several other states, spreading the timeless teachings of Vedanta and inspiring countless seekers on the path of spiritual knowledge.

Her gentle yet resolute leadership has been instrumental in building Chinmayaranyam into the vibrant spiritual centre it is today — a place where devotion, education, and service come together in harmony.

Spiritual Journey

• • •

Early Life & Brahmacharini Days

From a young age, Mataji was drawn to spiritual pursuits and the timeless wisdom of the scriptures. Inspired by Pujya Gurudev Swami Chinmayananda, she embraced the path of Brahmacharya and dedicated herself to conducting Jnana Yagnas across multiple states.

Jnana Yagnas & Discourses

Mataji conducted Jnana Yagnas (spiritual discourses) not only in Andhra Pradesh but also in other states, spreading the light of Vedantic knowledge to thousands of seekers. Her eloquent and heartfelt discourses continue to inspire devotees to this day.

Establishing Chinmayaranyam

With unwavering determination, Mataji established and developed Chinmayaranyam Ashram near Tirupati — creating a sanctuary for spiritual seekers, a centre for Vedantic study, and a hub for community service in the sacred shadow of Tirumala.

Compassion in Action

Beyond spiritual teaching, Mataji's compassion is reflected in programs like Bala Vihar for children, CHYK for youth, daily Annadanam, educational support for the underprivileged, and welfare programs that uplift countless lives.

Her Vision

A Vision for the Future

Mataji envisions Chinmayaranyam as a thriving centre where ancient wisdom meets modern needs — a place where every individual, regardless of background, can experience the transformative power of spiritual knowledge.

Her vision encompasses nurturing children through Bala Vihar, empowering youth through CHYK, expanding educational opportunities, and continuing the sacred traditions of worship and service that define Chinmayaranyam.

  • Bala Vihar for children (ages 5–18)
  • Chinmaya Yuva Kendra (CHYK) for youth (ages 18–30)
  • Strengthening Vidyalaya & educational programs
  • Expanding Jnana Yagnas & community seva
